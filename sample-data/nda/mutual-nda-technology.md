## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT — TECHNOLOGY & INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** ("Effective Date") by and between:

**[Party A Name]**, a [jurisdiction] corporation with its principal place of business at [address] ("Party A"), and

**[Party B Name]**, a [jurisdiction] corporation with its principal place of business at [address] ("Party B").

---

### 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential technology partnership, joint development, or integration project concerning **[Description of Purpose]** (the "Purpose") and, in connection therewith, each Party may disclose certain Confidential Information, including proprietary technology, source code, algorithms, and technical data to the other Party.

### 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by either Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), including but not limited to:

- **Source code**, object code, and software documentation
- **Algorithms**, data models, and machine learning models
- **API specifications**, architecture diagrams, and system designs
- **Technical data**, research results, and experimental findings
- **Patent applications** and invention disclosures (whether filed or unfiled)
- **Trade secrets** and proprietary know-how
- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Customer data and usage analytics
- Any information marked as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or "Restricted"

### 3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information, as documented by development logs or version control records;
(d) Is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;
(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice (at least 10 business days where possible) to the Disclosing Party.

### 4. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
(c) Limit access to Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a direct need to know and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those herein;
(d) Protect the Confidential Information using industry-standard security measures, including but not limited to encryption at rest and in transit, access controls, and audit logging;
(e) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party (within 48 hours) of any unauthorized disclosure, access, or use of the Confidential Information;
(f) Maintain a log of all personnel who have accessed the Confidential Information.

### 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY PROTECTIONS

(a) **Ownership.** All Confidential Information and any intellectual property rights therein remain the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. Nothing in this Agreement transfers or assigns any intellectual property rights.
(b) **No Reverse Engineering.** The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, algorithms, or underlying structure of any software, technology, or products disclosed as Confidential Information.
(c) **No Derivative Works.** The Receiving Party shall not create derivative works based on the Confidential Information without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.
(d) **Pre-existing IP.** Each Party retains all rights in its pre-existing intellectual property. Nothing in this Agreement grants a license to any pre-existing IP.

### 6. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain the following security measures:

(a) **Encryption:** All Confidential Information stored electronically must be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent;
(b) **Access Controls:** Role-based access controls with multi-factor authentication;
(c) **Network Security:** Confidential Information must not be stored on publicly accessible systems;
(d) **Audit Trail:** Maintain audit logs of access to Confidential Information for the duration of this Agreement plus two (2) years;
(e) **Incident Response:** Maintain a documented security incident response plan.

### 7. TERM

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of **[Term: 3 years]** from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination for a period of **[Survival Period: 3 years]** following the date of termination or expiration, or indefinitely for trade secrets.

### 8. RESIDUALS

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, either Party shall be free to use for any purpose the residuals resulting from access to or work with the Confidential Information, provided that such Party does not disclose the Confidential Information. "Residuals" means information in intangible form, which is retained in the unaided memory of persons who have had authorized access to the Confidential Information, **excluding** any source code, algorithms, data models, or specific technical implementations.

### 9. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF MATERIALS

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon written request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall within **fifteen (15) business days**:

(a) Return or securely destroy all Confidential Information, including all copies, backups, and derivatives;
(b) Permanently delete all electronic copies from all systems, including development environments, staging servers, and cloud storage;
(c) Provide a written certification of destruction signed by an authorized officer.

### 10. NO LICENSE OR WARRANTY

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any rights, license, or interest in the Confidential Information except the limited right to use it for the Purpose. ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED.

### 11. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

### 12. INDEMNIFICATION

The Receiving Party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Disclosing Party from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from any breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its personnel.

### 13. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **[Governing Law: State of California]**, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any disputes arising out of this Agreement shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts located in **[Jurisdiction: San Francisco, CA]**.

### 14. GENERAL PROVISIONS

(a) **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning the subject matter hereof.
(b) **Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.
(c) **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party.
(d) **Severability.** If any provision is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
(e) **Notice.** All notices under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth above.
