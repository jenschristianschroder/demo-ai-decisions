# Legal Playbook — Contract Terms and Negotiation Guidelines

**Last Reviewed:** February 8, 2026
**Owner:** Patricia Osei, General Counsel
**Approved Use:** Internal use only by Legal, Sales, and Deal Desk teams during contract negotiations. This document is attorney work product and should not be shared with customers or prospects. Contract terms described herein represent Northstar's standard and fallback positions.

---

## Limitation of Liability

### Standard Position
Total aggregate liability is **capped at 12 months of fees** paid or payable under the agreement.

### Common Customer Requests and Responses

| Customer Request | Northstar Position | Approval Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Liability capped at 12 months fees | ✅ **Standard — pre-approved** | None |
| Liability capped at 24 months fees | Acceptable fallback | **General Counsel** |
| Liability capped at total contract value | Acceptable for multi-year deals | **General Counsel** |
| **Unlimited liability for data breaches** | ❌ **NOT APPROVED** | N/A |
| Liability for data breaches capped at 2x annual fees | Maximum approved position for data breach carve-out | **General Counsel + CEO** |

**Key Principle:** Northstar does not accept unlimited liability under any circumstances. The maximum approved liability for data breach carve-outs is **2x annual fees**, and this position requires both General Counsel and CEO approval.

## Source Code Escrow

Northstar offers source code escrow through **Iron Mountain** (Intellectual Property Management division):

- **Escrow agent:** Iron Mountain
- **Deposit frequency:** Updated within 30 days of each major release
- **Release conditions:** Northstar ceases to operate, fails to maintain the software, or enters bankruptcy/receivership
- **Verification:** Customers may request escrow deposit verification annually at their cost
- **Cost:** Escrow arrangement fee is borne by the customer ($5,000 setup + $2,500/year maintenance, paid directly to Iron Mountain)

Source code escrow is available upon request and is included as a standard option in Enterprise-tier agreements.

## Indemnification

### Standard Position
Northstar provides indemnification for third-party claims arising from:
1. Intellectual property infringement (see IP Infringement section below)
2. Northstar's negligence or willful misconduct
3. Northstar's breach of confidentiality obligations

**Indemnification cap:** Total indemnification obligations are **capped at the total contract value** (total fees paid or payable over the contract term).

### Common Customer Requests and Responses

| Customer Request | Northstar Position | Approval Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Indemnification capped at contract value | ✅ **Standard — pre-approved** | None |
| Indemnification capped at 2x contract value | Acceptable fallback | **General Counsel** |
| **Uncapped indemnification** | ❌ **NOT APPROVED** | N/A |
| Mutual indemnification | ✅ Standard (mutual for IP infringement and confidentiality) | None |

**Key Principle:** Uncapped indemnification is **never approved**. The maximum fallback position is 2x contract value, requiring General Counsel approval.

## Intellectual Property Infringement Indemnity

Northstar indemnifies customers against third-party claims that the Northstar platform infringes valid US patents, copyrights, or trade secrets.

- **Cap:** IP infringement indemnity is **capped at 1x annual fees**
- **Exceptions:** Indemnity does not apply to claims arising from (a) customer modifications to the platform, (b) use of the platform in combination with non-Northstar products, or (c) use of the platform in violation of the agreement
- **Remediation:** If the platform is found to infringe, Northstar may (at its option) (i) obtain a license, (ii) modify the platform to be non-infringing, or (iii) terminate the agreement and refund prepaid fees for the unused term

| Customer Request | Northstar Position | Approval Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| IP indemnity capped at 1x annual fees | ✅ **Standard — pre-approved** | None |
| IP indemnity capped at 2x annual fees | Acceptable fallback | **General Counsel** |
| Uncapped IP indemnity | ❌ **NOT APPROVED** | N/A |

## Termination for Convenience

### Standard Position
Either party may terminate the agreement for convenience with **90 calendar days' written notice**.

### Common Customer Requests and Responses

| Customer Request | Northstar Position | Approval Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 90-day termination for convenience (mutual) | ✅ **Standard — pre-approved** | None |
| 60-day termination for convenience | Acceptable fallback | **General Counsel** |
| 30-day termination for convenience | Not recommended; acceptable only for month-to-month agreements | **General Counsel + CRO** |
| Termination for convenience with refund of prepaid fees | Standard for annual prepayments (pro-rated refund) | None |
| Termination for convenience with no refund | Not standard; only for heavily discounted deals | **CFO** |

### Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate for material breach if the breach remains uncured for 30 days after written notice. Termination for cause is standard and non-negotiable.

## Cyber Insurance

Northstar maintains the following cyber insurance coverage:

| Coverage | Amount | Carrier |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Cyber Liability (including data breach) | **$5,000,000** | Chubb |
| Errors & Omissions (Technology E&O) | $5,000,000 | Chubb |
| General Commercial Liability | $2,000,000 | Hartford |

### Requests to Increase Coverage

| Customer Request | Northstar Position | Approval Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Evidence of $5M cyber insurance | ✅ Standard — certificate of insurance available upon request | None |
| Increase to $10M cyber insurance | Requires policy amendment | **Board of Directors** |
| Increase above $10M | Not feasible under current policy structure | N/A |
| Customer named as additional insured | Available upon request (standard endorsement) | **General Counsel** |

**Key Principle:** Requests to increase cyber insurance to $10M require **Board of Directors** approval due to the premium and policy implications. Sales teams should not commit to increased coverage without confirming feasibility with Legal.

## Data Protection and Confidentiality

### Standard Terms
- Customer data remains the property of the customer at all times
- Northstar processes customer data only as directed by the customer and as necessary to provide the service
- Upon termination, customer data is returned (in standard export format) and deleted from Northstar systems within 60 days
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination for 3 years
- Northstar employees with access to customer data are subject to background checks and confidentiality agreements

---

## Constraints and Caveats

- This playbook represents Northstar's negotiation positions as approved by Legal and executive leadership. Positions described as "NOT APPROVED" are firm and should not be offered under any circumstances.
- Fallback positions requiring General Counsel or CEO approval should be escalated before being communicated to the customer, not after.
- Contract terms for government customers may be subject to mandatory government contract clauses (e.g., FAR/DFARS) that override standard positions. Government contracts should always involve Legal review.
- Cyber insurance coverage amounts are current as of the last policy renewal (July 2025). Coverage is reviewed annually.
- Source code escrow costs are borne by the customer and paid directly to Iron Mountain. Northstar does not subsidize escrow fees.

## Suggested RFP Response Language

> Northstar's standard contract terms include limitation of liability capped at 12 months of fees, mutual indemnification, and termination for convenience with 90 days' notice. Source code escrow is available through Iron Mountain. Northstar maintains $5 million in cyber liability insurance and $5 million in technology errors and omissions coverage. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for customers with data protection requirements. Detailed contract terms are negotiated as part of the contracting process, and Northstar's Legal team is available to engage directly with customer procurement and legal counsel.
