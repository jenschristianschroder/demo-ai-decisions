## Discount Policy

### Term-Based Discounts

| Contract Term | Discount |
|--------------|----------|
| 1-year | List price (no discount) |
| **3-year** | **10%** off subscription fees |
| **5-year** | **15%** off subscription fees |

Term-based discounts apply to subscription fees only, not implementation or training fees.

### Government Discount

An additional **8% discount** is available for federal, state, and local government agencies. This discount is stackable with term-based discounts.

**Example:** A 3-year government contract receives 10% term discount + 8% government discount = approximately 17.2% effective discount.

Government discount eligibility requires verification of government entity status.

### Volume Discounts

Volume discounts are available for deployments exceeding **300 users**:

| User Count | Additional Discount |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 300–499 users | 3% |
| 500–999 users | 5% |
| 1,000–2,499 users | 8% |
| 2,500+ users | Custom negotiation |

Volume discounts are stackable with term-based and government discounts.

### Discount Approval Matrix

| Discount Type | Threshold | Approval Required |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Term-based (10% or 15%) | Pre-approved | None |
| Government (8%) | Pre-approved with verification | Deal Desk |
| Volume (per schedule above) | Pre-approved | Deal Desk |
| Combined discounts exceeding 25% | Non-standard | **VP of Sales** |
| Combined discounts exceeding 35% | Non-standard | **CRO** |
| Any discount on implementation fees | Non-standard | **VP of Professional Services** |

### Most-Favored-Customer (MFC) Clause

A most-favored-customer clause is **NOT standard Northstar policy**. MFC clauses guarantee that the customer receives pricing no less favorable than any other customer, which creates significant commercial risk.

- MFC requests must be escalated to and approved by the **CEO**
- MFC clauses have been approved in fewer than 5 engagements in company history
- Sales teams should offer alternative value (additional training, extended hypercare, premium support upgrade) rather than agreeing to MFC terms
- If an MFC clause is approved, it must be limited in scope (e.g., same tier, same user count range, same term length) and duration (e.g., initial term only, not renewals)

## Constraints and Caveats

- Enterprise tier pricing ($110/user/month) is a list price starting point; actual pricing is negotiable for large or strategic accounts.
- Implementation pricing ($150,000 standard) is an estimate. Actual implementation costs are scoped during Discovery and documented in a Statement of Work.
- Discounts on implementation and training fees are non-standard and require VP approval.
- Government discount eligibility must be verified by Deal Desk before including in proposals.
- Most-favored-customer clauses require CEO approval and should not be offered or implied during sales conversations without prior authorization.
- Pricing information is confidential and should not be published in written RFP responses. Provide pricing in a separate, sealed pricing proposal per RFP instructions.
