# Vendor Service Agreement

**Agreement Number:** VSA-2026-0847
**Effective Date:** 1 July 2026
**Expiration Date:** 30 June 2029

## Parties

1. **Client:** Maersk Global Logistics A/S, Esplanaden 50, 1098 Copenhagen K, Denmark ("Client")
2. **Vendor:** OceanTech Solutions Ltd., 45 Maritime Way, Singapore 049315 ("Vendor")

## 1. Definitions

1.1 "Services" means the IT infrastructure monitoring, predictive maintenance analytics, and vessel tracking software services described in Schedule A.

1.2 "Confidential Information" means all proprietary information, trade secrets, business data, customer data, and technical information disclosed by either party.

1.3 "Service Level" means the performance standards set forth in Schedule B.

1.4 "Data" means all information processed, stored, or transmitted by the Vendor in the course of providing the Services.

1.5 "Intellectual Property" means all patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, and other proprietary rights.

## 2. Scope of Services

2.1 The Vendor shall provide the Services as described in Schedule A, including:
- Real-time vessel monitoring and tracking
- Predictive maintenance analytics for fleet operations
- IT infrastructure monitoring for port operations
- Data integration with Client's existing ERP and logistics systems

2.2 The Vendor shall perform the Services in accordance with industry best practices and the Service Levels set forth in Schedule B.

## 3. Term and Termination

3.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of three (3) years unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.

3.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party.

3.3 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party commits a material breach that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice.

3.4 Upon termination, the Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not exceeding six (6) months at the Vendor's then-current rates.

## 4. Payment Terms

4.1 The Client shall pay the Vendor in accordance with the fee schedule set forth in Schedule C.

4.2 All invoices shall be paid within forty-five (45) days of receipt.

4.3 Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 2% per month on the outstanding balance.

4.4 The Vendor may suspend Services if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days.

## 5. Liability and Indemnification

5.1 The Vendor's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

5.2 In no event shall either party be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

5.3 The Vendor shall indemnify and hold harmless the Client against all third-party claims arising from the Vendor's negligence or willful misconduct in providing the Services.

5.4 The Client shall indemnify and hold harmless the Vendor against all claims arising from the Client's use of the Services in violation of applicable law.

## 6. Confidentiality

6.1 Each party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of the other party's Confidential Information for a period of five (5) years following disclosure.

6.2 Confidential Information may be disclosed to employees and subcontractors on a need-to-know basis, provided they are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those set forth herein.

6.3 The obligations of confidentiality shall not apply to information that: (a) is publicly available; (b) was known prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law.

## 7. Data Protection

7.1 The Vendor shall process personal data only in accordance with the Client's documented instructions and applicable data protection laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

7.2 The Vendor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk of processing.

7.3 The Vendor may transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area only with the Client's prior written consent and subject to appropriate safeguards.

7.4 Upon termination of this Agreement, the Vendor shall delete or return all personal data within thirty (30) days, at the Client's election.

## 8. Intellectual Property

8.1 All intellectual property rights in materials developed by the Vendor specifically for the Client under this Agreement shall vest in the Client upon payment in full.

8.2 The Vendor retains all rights in its pre-existing intellectual property and general tools, methodologies, and know-how.

8.3 The Vendor grants the Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use the Vendor's pre-existing IP embedded in the deliverables.

## 9. Force Majeure

9.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure or delay in performing its obligations due to circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemics, government actions, or failures of third-party telecommunications.

9.2 The affected party shall notify the other party within five (5) business days of becoming aware of a force majeure event.

9.3 If a force majeure event continues for more than ninety (90) days, either party may terminate this Agreement without liability.

## 10. Dispute Resolution

10.1 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation under the rules of the Singapore International Mediation Centre.

10.2 If mediation fails within sixty (60) days, the dispute shall be finally resolved by arbitration under the SIAC Rules, with three arbitrators, seated in Singapore.

10.3 The language of the arbitration shall be English.

10.4 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Singapore.

## 11. Insurance

11.1 The Vendor shall maintain professional indemnity insurance with a minimum coverage of USD 5,000,000 per occurrence.

11.2 The Vendor shall maintain cyber liability insurance with a minimum coverage of USD 10,000,000 per occurrence.

11.3 The Vendor shall provide certificates of insurance upon request.

## 12. Compliance

12.1 Each party shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

12.2 The Vendor shall comply with the Client's Supplier Code of Conduct, a copy of which has been provided to the Vendor.

12.3 The Vendor represents that it has implemented appropriate anti-bribery and anti-corruption measures.

## 13. Warranty

13.1 The Vendor warrants that the Services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

13.2 The Vendor warrants that the Services shall conform to the specifications set forth in Schedule A.

13.3 The Vendor does not warrant that the Services will be uninterrupted or error-free.
